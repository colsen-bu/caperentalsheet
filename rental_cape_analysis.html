2. Market Analysis: The September Opportunity

Setting the right price requires understanding the specific market conditions for a late September weekend on Cape Cod. This is not peak summer, but it's a high-demand "shoulder season," further influenced by the nature of the rental. Here are the key factors that influence the recommended rate.

Shoulder Season Demand

Late September is a prime time for weddings and fall getaways on the Cape. While not as crowded as July or August, demand for high-quality properties remains strong. Rates are typically 10-20% lower than peak summer but significantly higher than the deep off-season.

The Wedding Premium

Rentals for events like weddings command a premium. This accounts for increased wear and tear and the higher value placed on a property that serves as a central hub for a major life event. A 15-30% event premium over the standard rate is typical in the market.
